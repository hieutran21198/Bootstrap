## Executive Summary

The current config is directionally sound (premium reasoning for orchestrator/architect, open-weight coding models for implementation lanes) but has three concrete problems: (1) the **explorer/researcher are on MiniMax with 192K context — the smallest window in the whole inventory — while cheaper 1M-context options exist**; (2) several inventory IDs are **retired or deprecating within weeks** (Claude Opus 4/Sonnet 4 retired 2026-06-15; GPT-5.1-codex line dies 2026-07-23; `deepseek-chat`/`deepseek-reasoner` die 2026-07-24; Kimi K2.5, MiniMax M2.5, Claude Opus 4.1 die 2026-08-05); (3) the scribe runs `deepseek-v4-pro` at `max` reasoning effort, which pays flagship-coding-model thinking budgets to write markdown.

- **Best overall default:** `anthropic/claude-sonnet-5` — 1M context, near-Opus agentic quality, $2/$10 intro pricing through 2026-08-31 (then $3/$15) ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-sonnet-5))
- **Best premium reasoning model:** `anthropic/claude-fable-5` ($10/$50, 1M ctx, SOTA nearly everywhere; slower and 2× Opus price), with `anthropic/claude-opus-4-8` as the rational premium default ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-8))
- **Best coding model (value):** `opencode-go/deepseek-v4-pro` (open-weight SOTA agentic coding, 1M ctx; $1.74/$3.48 via Zen, $0.435/$0.87 vendor-direct) — with `opencode-go/glm-5.2` (Terminal-Bench 2.1: 81.0) and `opencode-go/kimi-k2.7-code` as peers ([DeepSeek](https://api-docs.deepseek.com/news/news260424), [Z.ai](https://docs.z.ai/guides/llm/glm-5.2), [Moonshot](https://platform.kimi.ai/docs/guide/kimi-k2-7-code-quickstart))
- **Best cheap model:** `opencode-go/deepseek-v4-flash` — 1M context at $0.14/$0.28, near-Pro on simple agent tasks ([DeepSeek pricing](https://api-docs.deepseek.com/quick_start/pricing))
- **Best research model:** `opencode-go/qwen3.7-plus` for cost-balanced research (1M ctx, $0.40/$1.60, structured output); `openai/gpt-5.5` for premium synthesis (BrowseComp 84.4 at xhigh) ([Alibaba](https://help.aliyun.com/zh/model-studio/text-generation-model), [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/))
- **Biggest risks:** imminent deprecations (above); free models train on your data (all five `*-free`/stealth Zen models); the Zen gateway charges ~4× DeepSeek's vendor price for V4-Pro; Sonnet 5's price rises 50% on 2026-09-01; `opencode-go` has a 5-hour rolling usage cap that can stall long agent sessions
- **Recommended default preset: Preset B — Balanced Daily** (below), plus Preset D's escalation triggers as standing operating rules.

## Model Family Findings

### Anthropic Claude

#### Strengths

Best-in-class agentic reliability and long-horizon focus (Fable 5 sustains >30h; Sonnet 5 "stays on plan, follows conventions"); 1M context across the current lineup; the `effort` parameter (low→max) maps cleanly onto opencode's `variant`; strongest alignment/low-deception profile of the inventory (Opus 4.8, Fable 5).

#### Weaknesses

Price ceiling (Fable 5 $10/$50); Fable 5 is the slowest model in the lineup; the 2026-v2 tokenizer (4.7+) produces ~30% more tokens for the same text — real cost impact; conservative cyber/bio classifiers can flag benign security work (relevant for security-reviewer!); `-fast` only truly exists on Opus 4.8 (and 4.7 until 2026-07-24), and is a **no-op on 4.6**.

#### Best roles

orchestrator (Fable 5 / Opus 4.8), architect and security-reviewer (Opus 4.8 xhigh), backend-engineer for the hardest refactors, frontend/scribe/explorer on Sonnet 5.

#### Avoid for

High-volume cheap lanes on Opus/Fable (cost); anything on retired Opus 4 / Sonnet 4 or deprecated Opus 4.1; Haiku 4.5 for architecture or synthesis.

#### Cost/performance notes

Sonnet 5 at $2/$10 intro (→ $3/$15 from 2026-09-01) is the best price/performance point of the **entire inventory** among frontier-quality models. Opus 4.8 $5/$25. Fable 5 $10/$50. Haiku 4.5 $1/$5. Zen adds a >200K surcharge to Sonnet 4.5 only. Note: this workspace has `opencode-claude-auth` installed globally — if Anthropic usage is subscription-billed rather than per-token, premium Anthropic picks are cheaper in practice than list price suggests (medium confidence; verify billing).

### OpenAI GPT / Codex

#### Strengths

GPT-5.5 and 5.4 are top-tier reasoning models with 1.05M context and the richest hosted-tool surface; effort ladder none→xhigh gives fine cost control; GPT-5.4 explicitly absorbed the codex line's coding skills ("simplify the choice between models"); `-pro` tiers are the strongest available "second-opinion oracle" for final review.

#### Weaknesses

Nine of the workspace's OpenAI IDs are deprecated upstream — the entire GPT-5.1-codex line plus gpt-5-codex and gpt-5.2-codex **shut down 2026-07-23**; `-pro` has no streaming, multi-minute latency, and 6× cost for a 2–4pt eval gain; the three `openai/*-fast` IDs have no public model pages (likely Fast mode: 1.5× speed at 2.5× cost — unverified); >272K input costs 2×.

#### Best roles

architect and security-reviewer (gpt-5.5 xhigh — current assignment, validated); backend-engineer via gpt-5.3-codex or gpt-5.4; researcher (gpt-5.5 high); release-engineer (gpt-5.4, reliable and literal).

#### Avoid for

Interactive/subagent lanes on `-pro` (latency, no streaming); anything on the 5.1-codex/5-codex/5.2-codex line (18-day shutdown clock); explorer on anything OpenAI-premium (cost).

#### Cost/performance notes

gpt-5.5 $5/$30 · 5.5-pro $30/$180 · 5.4 $2.50/$15 · 5.4-mini $0.75/$4.50 · 5.4-nano $0.20/$1.25 · 5.3-codex $1.75/$14 · 5.2 $1.75/$14. gpt-5.4 is the best OpenAI cost/capability point. `opencode-openai-codex-auth` is installed globally — same subscription-billing caveat as Anthropic (medium confidence).

### DeepSeek

#### Strengths

V4-Pro: open-source SOTA agentic-coding claim, 1M context (DSA sparse attention), world-class reasoning at open-weight prices; V4-Flash: near-Pro on simple agent tasks at $0.14/$0.28 — the best cheap 1M-context model in the inventory; excellent cache-hit economics ($0.0028/Mtok cached vendor-side).

#### Weaknesses

`deepseek-chat`/`deepseek-reasoner` aliases **retire 2026-07-24**; Zen charges ~4× DeepSeek's own price for V4-Pro ($1.74/$3.48 vs $0.435/$0.87) — vendor-direct keys are configured in this workspace's global opencode config and are the cheaper route; V4-Pro concurrency capped at 500; can overthink without effort control.

#### Best roles

backend-engineer / release-engineer (V4-Pro — current assignments, validated); explorer (V4-Flash); frontend-engineer (V4-Pro medium or Flash).

#### Avoid for

Nothing structural — but never start new work on the `deepseek-chat`/`reasoner` aliases, and don't route secrets through `deepseek-v4-flash-free` (trains on data).

#### Cost/performance notes

V4-Flash $0.14/$0.28 (identical vendor and Zen). V4-Pro $0.435/$0.87 vendor vs $1.74/$3.48 Zen — consider `deepseek/deepseek-v4-pro` (vendor-native, API key already provisioned via sops) over `opencode-go/deepseek-v4-pro` where the Go subscription cap isn't the point.

### Gemini

#### Strengths

Whole family serves 1M context; 3-flash is very cheap ($0.50/$3.00) and fast for long sweeps; 3.1-pro is a credible long-context research/analysis model.

#### Weaknesses

Capability scores are inferential this cycle — Google's launch posts were unreachable during research (404/auth-blocked), so no first-party benchmark data is in hand; 3.1-pro doubles price above 200K input; Flash tiers are shallow for multi-step synthesis; older `gemini-3-pro` was already deprecated on Zen (2026-03-09) — the family churns.

#### Best roles

explorer / researcher pre-filtering (3-flash, 3.5-flash); researcher long-context synthesis (3.1-pro).

#### Avoid for

## Variant Findings

Variant names are **not** portable across providers. Two different things get called "variant" here: (a) **model-ID suffixes** (different SKU/serving tier), and (b) the opencode **`variant` config field**, which maps to the vendor's reasoning-effort knob for the *same* checkpoint (Anthropic `effort`/thinking budget: `high`/`max` built-in; OpenAI `reasoningEffort`: `none`/`minimal`/`low`/`medium`/`high`/`xhigh`; Google: `low`/`high`) — it is never a different model ([opencode models docs](https://opencode.ai/docs/models#variants)).

- **`nano`** (OpenAI only): cheapest SKU ($0.20/$1.25 for 5.4-nano); bulk classification/routing; too shallow for reasoning work.
- **`mini`** (OpenAI): mid-size sibling for "responsive coding tasks and subagents" (5.4-mini: 400K ctx, $0.75/$4.50); a real workhorse tier, unlike nano.
- **`flash`** (DeepSeek, Gemini): the fast/cheap serving of the family, but *radically* different quality by vendor — DeepSeek V4-Flash is a near-Pro 1M-context 13B-active MoE at $0.14/$0.28, while Gemini Flash is a speed-over-depth tier. "Flash" ≠ weak; check per vendor.
- **`fast`** (Anthropic, OpenAI): **same weights, faster serving, premium price.** Anthropic: `speed:"fast"` + beta header, 2.5× output TPS at 2× price, only on Opus 4.8 (4.7-fast dies 2026-07-24; on 4.6 it's a silent no-op billed at standard rates). OpenAI: "Fast mode" 1.5× speed at 2.5× cost; the `openai/*-fast` IDs have no public model pages — low confidence, verify before use.
- **default/base**: the standard serving of the checkpoint; with reasoning models, the effective quality is set by the `variant` (effort) field, so "base at xhigh" can beat "pro at low" per dollar.
- **`pro`**: two meanings. OpenAI `-pro` = compute-scaled oracle (6× price, no streaming, minutes of latency). DeepSeek/MiMo `pro` = the bigger MoE sibling of `flash`/base (normal serving). Do not conflate.
- **`max`**: also two meanings. Qwen `3.7-max` = flagship SKU above `plus`. Anthropic/opencode `variant = "max"` = maximum thinking budget on the same checkpoint. (GPT-5.1-codex-**max** was a SKU; dead 2026-07-23.)
- **`codex`** (OpenAI): agentic-coding specialist checkpoints (no `none` effort). Post GPT-5.4, mainline models absorbed codex coding skills — `-codex` is now a niche, not the default coding answer. Only 5.3-codex is current; everything older shuts down 2026-07-23.
- **`spark`** (OpenAI): research-preview, text-only, near-instant coding iteration on the ChatGPT-Pro surface; no public API pricing; not fit for agent lanes.
- **free variants** (Zen): $0 tiers where **your prompts may train the model**; undocumented rate limits, no SLA; suitable only for explicitly experimental, non-sensitive work.

## Operating Rules

1. **Cheap models are allowed for low-risk local exploration** — explorer/dev-environment may run flash-tier models freely; findings are verified by whoever consumes them.
2. **A stronger model is required for architecture and cross-boundary decisions** — anything producing or reviewing an ADR/spec runs gpt-5.5 (xhigh) or Opus 4.8 (xhigh) class. No open-weight budget model accepts an ADR.
3. **The strongest available model is required for final review of risky implementation** (migrations, RLS/auth, cross-module refactors, release candidates) — and the reviewer's model family should differ from the implementer's where practical (writer ≠ reviewer, extended to model space).
4. **Researcher must cite sources** — every claim carries a URL; a researcher on a budget model that cannot sustain citation discipline gets escalated, not excused. Free models are banned for research (prompts may train third-party models).
5. **Scribe optimizes for structured writing and consistency** — prefer models with verified structured-output support (qwen3.7-plus, Claude/GPT lines); never `qwen3.7-max` for schema-bound work until its structured-output gap is disproven.
6. **Release-engineer favors reliability over creativity** — literal models (gpt-5.4, Kimi K2.6, DeepSeek V4-Pro at moderate effort); no free models, no alpha/stealth models, ever, in a release path.
7. **Frontend-engineer must not use weak models for visual/UX-heavy work** — scaffold edits can stay on the budget pick; anything judged on design taste escalates to Sonnet 5+.
8. **Backend-engineer escalates for migrations, auth, RLS, concurrency, security, or large refactors** — per the Preset D trigger table; the security-reviewer independently audits DB-touching changes regardless of implementation model.
9. **Orchestrator uses a model strong enough to decompose, route, and detect when to delegate** — never below Sonnet 5 class. A weak orchestrator silently degrades every other lane.
10. **Humans keep authority** — PRD acceptance, priority/sprint scope, DoD sign-off, and release go/no-go are human gates (Rule 5, `implementation-workflow.md`); model changes themselves are config changes requiring human approval.
11. **Deprecation hygiene** — check the [Zen deprecation table](https://opencode.ai/docs/zen) and vendor deprecation pages before pinning any model in config; nothing with a shutdown date inside 60 days gets a new assignment.
